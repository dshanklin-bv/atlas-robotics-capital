01The Thesis

In 1973, Steven Udvar-Házy saw that airlines couldn't afford the new jet age. He built a $35 billion empire by buying aircraft and leasing them to the world.

Today, the same opportunity exists in humanoid robotics.

The humanoid robot revolution is here — but businesses can't afford the capital expenditure. Atlas Robotics Capital bridges the gap between revolutionary technology and practical adoption. We acquire humanoid robots at scale, then lease them to enterprises on terms that make economic sense.

It's not a new idea. It's a proven idea, applied to the defining technology of our century.

Senior Tranche (70%)

Backed by the hard asset value of the robots and predictable lease cash flows. Insurance-wrapped. Think of it as the mortgage — low risk, steady income.

Mezzanine (15%)

Subordinated debt with enhanced yield. Absorbs losses after equity is exhausted. Attractive for institutional investors seeking yield with asset backing.

Equity (15%)

First-loss position with the highest upside. Captures residual value, fleet expansion gains, and the operating margin of the management company.

07The Precedent

The Udvar-Házy Playbook

1973

Founded International Lease Finance Corporation (ILFC) with $150,000 in startup capital.

1987

230 aircraft, serving 70 airlines across the globe. Run by just 16 employees.

1990

Sold ILFC to AIG for $1.26 billion. Continued to run the company for two more decades.

2010

Founded Air Lease Corporation. Grew it to a multi-billion dollar public company.

Today

Aircraft leasing is a $187 billion industry. The model is proven, scalable, and enduring.

We're applying the same playbook to robotics.
