# Atlas Robotics Capital

> **The Udvar-Házy Model for the Robotics Age**

![Concept](./assets/atlas-robotics-hero.png)

## Overview

Atlas Robotics Capital is a conceptual business plan for a **humanoid robot leasing company** modeled after Steven Udvar-Házy's revolutionary aircraft leasing empire (ILFC → AerCap / Air Lease Corporation).

### The Core Thesis

In 1973, Udvar-Házy recognized that airlines couldn't afford the capital cost of jet aircraft — yet they desperately needed them. He built a $35 billion empire by buying aircraft and leasing them to the world. **Today, the same structural opportunity exists in humanoid robotics.**

- Businesses need humanoid robots priced at $20,000–$250,000
- Most SMEs cannot afford the capital expenditure
- The leasing model converts capex to opex — just like aircraft leasing did for airlines
- A diversified portfolio of robots from multiple OEMs reduces single-company risk
- Structured finance (ABS tranches) allows investors to choose their risk/return profile

## The Business Model

### How It Works

```
BUY → LEASE → MANAGE → SECURITIZE → REPEAT
```

1. **BUY**: Purchase humanoid robots from 10+ OEMs (1X, Figure, Agility, Tesla, Boston Dynamics, Unitree) at volume discounts
2. **LEASE**: Deploy to businesses on 3–5 year full-service operating leases ($2,000–$8,000/robot/month)
3. **MANAGE**: Handle maintenance, software updates, and fleet operations via centralized platform
4. **SECURITIZE**: Package lease receivables into tranched asset-backed securities for investors
5. **REPEAT**: Recycle proceeds into new robot purchases, compounding the fleet

### Investment Structure (Tranched ABS)

| Tranche | % of Pool | Target Rating | Target Yield | Risk Profile |
|---------|-----------|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Senior (Class A) | 65% | A / AA | SOFR + 200–300 bps | Lowest risk; last to absorb losses |
| Mezzanine (Class B) | 15% | BBB | SOFR + 400–600 bps | Moderate risk; enhanced yield |
| Subordinate (Class C) | 10% | BB | SOFR + 800–1,100 bps | Higher risk; structural support |
| Equity (Class E) | 10% | Unrated | 20–30% IRR | First loss; highest potential return |
